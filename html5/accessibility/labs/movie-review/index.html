Movie Review: Wing It!

Blender Studio delivers a delightful and nimble 4-minute short film with WING IT!, a stylized mix of 2D and 3D animation that showcases the studio's expertise and Blender's flexibility. Centered on the quirky dynamic between a meticulous inventor-cat and his exuberant dog sidekick, the film explodes into slapstick chaos when their makeshift rocket misfires in spectacular cartoon fashion. It's charmingly visualized using non-photorealistic rendering techniques and experiments in NPR stylization, pushing Blender's rendering pipelines to new creative heights. Despite its short runtime, the pacing feels tight and fun, delivering humor, character, and motion without a wasted frame. As with all Blender Open Movies, production files, blend resources, and educational breakdowns are freely released—making it both entertaining and an invaluable learning resource for artists and developers alike. In short, WING IT! is a high-spirited technical showcase wrapped in a playful narrative—perfect for fans of open animation, Blender enthusiasts, or anyone craving a quick and creative burst of entertainment.

Movie Rating: ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐☆ (9/10)
